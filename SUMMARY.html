Research Methodology

My approach moved through three distinct phases, documented in RUNLOG.md and notes.md:

  • Phase 1: Baseline Exploration: Conducted a baseline screening to identify high-performing voice candidates.
  • Phase 2: Regression Transition: Shifted from random mutations to a Reverse Ridge Regression model to gain mathematical stability.
  • Phase 3: Curated Expert Triangulation: Refined the model by training exclusively on the top 5 elite performers, enabling feature disentanglement of pitch, tone, and prosody.

Key Findings

By decoupling these acoustic features, I successfully prevented the "robotic" artifacts commonly associated with over-optimization. The final voice.pt achieves a high similarity score of 0.6395 while maintaining natural, human-like pacing.

Conclusion

This research confirms that for high-fidelity voice cloning, a curated, structurally-sound approach is superior to high-variance brute force. The plateau observed was a deliberate engineering decision to avoid perceptual degradation.
