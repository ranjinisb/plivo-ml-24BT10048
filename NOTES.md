My fitness function utilizes a cosine similarity metric between the generated acoustic embedding and the target's reference embedding. My best result achieved a fitness score of 0.6395 using a curated expert regression model trained on the top 5 baseline voices. 
This targeted approach bypassed the noise of lower-scoring candidates, allowing the model to triangulate the target's unique pitch and timbre more effectively. Throughout the research, I transitioned from a brute-force random walk to a segmented ridge regression, which decoupled pitch and tone from the prosody. This shift was critical for preserving the natural human cadence of the speech. 
I observed that the similarity score plateaued at 0.6395 because further optimization led to overfitting, introducing metallic artifacts and high-frequency buzzing. Ultimately, I prioritized structural fidelity and perceptual naturalness over raw mathematical similarity. 
This research demonstrates that latent space optimization is most effective when features are disentangled and the training data is strategically curated.
